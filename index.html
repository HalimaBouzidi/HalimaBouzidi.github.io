Hi! My name is Halima Bouzidi ^^

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Embedded & Cyber-Physical Systems (AICPS) Lab at the University of California, Irvine (UCI), where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Mohammad Abdullah Al Faruque.

My research stands at the intersection of Multi-objective optimization, Machine Learning (ML), Hardware architectures, Security, and Privacy with an emphasis on resource-constrained intelligent systems. To enhance the performance, efficiency, and reliability of intelligent applications and devices, I develop methods that seamlessly integrate ML, optimization, security, and privacy analysis across both software and hardware levels.

I obtained my PhD degree in Computer Engineering in January 2024 from the Polytechnic University of Hauts-de-France (UPHF) where I was a member of the the LAMIH lab. I was fortunate to be advised by Prof. Smail NIAR, Prof. El-Ghazali Talbi, and Dr. Hamza Ouarnoughi.

Prior to my PhD program, in September 2020, I graduated with honors from the Higher school of Computer Science (ESI ex INI) in Algiers, Algeria, where I received both my Master’s and Engineering degrees. I was mentored and introduced to embedded systems by Prof. Mouloud Koudil and Dr. Nassima Kadri.
